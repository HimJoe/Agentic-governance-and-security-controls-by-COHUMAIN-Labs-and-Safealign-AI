# Contributing

This is an open standard — it improves through real-world use. Thank you for helping.

## Who we're looking for
- **Practitioners** building agent systems who can tell us where a control is impractical.
- **Security engineers** who red-team agents and can sharpen the threat mappings.
- **Governance & compliance leads** applying the standard in regulated settings.
- **Platform developers** wiring controls into runtime enforcement.

## How to contribute
1. **Open an issue** using the templates (control proposal, mapping correction, clarification).
2. **For a new or changed control**, include: the risk it closes (ideally a MIT AI Risk Repository subdomain), a testable objective, key requirements, the evidence that proves it, and proposed framework mappings.
3. **Submit a PR.** Update both the human-readable (`controls/CONTROLS.md`) and machine-readable (`controls/controls.json`, `controls/controls.csv`) sources, and the workbook if structural.
4. Keep mappings **defensible** — cite the framework version. Mark anything indicative.

## Principles to preserve
- Risk-first, testable, evidence-backed.
- Governance and security stay unified — don't silo them.
- Scoped, not exhaustive: macro/societal risks remain out of scope.
- Plain language; vendor-neutral.

## Governance
Changes are accepted by consensus of the maintainers (see `GOVERNANCE.md`). Versioning follows SemVer; see `CHANGELOG.md`.
